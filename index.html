◎ About

I am a PhD candidate in Electrical Engineering at the University of South Florida, working in the Nano Computing Research Group under Dr. Sanjukta Bhanja. My research sits at the intersection of neuromorphic computing, probabilistic machine learning, and hardware-software co-design.

I build systems that learn online, adapt to streaming data, and run efficiently on real hardware — from Hierarchical Temporal Memory architectures to spintronic processing-in-memory simulators. I care about uncertainty: knowing what a model doesn't know is often as important as what it does.

Before the PhD, I worked as a software engineer at Cognizant, which gave me an enduring appreciation for systems that have to work in the real world, not just on benchmarks.

✶ Research

Neuromorphic Sequence Modeling

Designing HTM- and Reflex-Memory-based architectures for streaming anomaly detection and forecasting, with a focus on scalability, latency, and biological plausibility.

Bayesian Deep Learning

Boosted variational inference for uncertainty-aware neural networks, with applications to decision-making under imperfect information and calibrated probabilistic predictions.

Hardware-Conscious AI

Co-designing algorithms and emerging memory devices — Domain Wall Memory, spintronic PIM — as accelerators for temporal learning and neural workloads.

✺ Projects

Multi-Agent Reflex Memory

A distributed anomaly detection framework where multiple Reflex-Memory agents coordinate over streaming data across domains including finance, IoT, and transportation.

Streaming · Agents · HTM · Anomaly Detection

SPIMulator

A simulator for Domain Wall Memory based processing-in-memory architectures, supporting matrix operations and neuromorphic workloads on spintronic devices.

Hardware · DWM · PIM · Spintronics

Boosted Variational BNNs

Bayesian neural networks with boosted variational inference on synthetic and real time-series data, emphasizing calibrated uncertainty and practical deployability.

Bayesian · Uncertainty · Time-series
